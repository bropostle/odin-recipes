Ice Cream

Description

This recipe is for delicious home made ice cream. Enjoy this wonderful summertime treat that will bring back memories of long days and late nights!

Ingredients

  • 10 large egg yolks
  • 1 cup (100 grams) granulated sugar, divided
  • 3 cups (720 milliliters) heavy cream
  • 2 cups (480 milliliters) whole milk
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3/4 teaspoon fine salt

Steps

  1. In a medium bowl, whisk the egg yolks and 1/2 cup (100 grams) of the sugar until well combined.
  2. In a large saucepan over medium-high heat, combine the cream, milk, the remaining 1/2 cup (100 grams) of the sugar, vanilla and salt and, stirring frequently to prevent scorching on the bottom, bring the mixture to a simmer, about 5 minutes. Remove from the heat.
  3. With a ladle in one hand and a whisk in the other, drizzle a small amount of the heated liquid into the egg mixture while whisking. Continue until about a third of the hot liquid has been blended with the eggs and the mixture feels warm to the touch. Slowly pour the egg mixture back into the pot, continuing to whisk until the custard is smooth and well combined.
  4. Return the saucepan to medium heat and let the custard come to a simmter, with small bubble around the edges; it should be thick enough to coat the back of a wooden spoon, or register about 170 degrees on an instant-read thermometer.
  5. Remove from the heat.
  6. Strain the mixture through a fine-mesh sieve into a large containter with a tightfitting lid, stirring and pressing the custard through with a silicone spatula or wooden spoon. Cover and refrigerate until thoroughly chilled, at least 6 hours and preferably overnight.
  7. Assemble your ice cream machine. Pour in the chilled custard and churn according to the manufacturer's instructions. The custard should be the consistency of soft-serve ice cream. Place the now-empty storage container in the freezer to chill while ice cream freezes.
  8. Pack the ice cream into the chilled storage container. Press a piece of wax paper directly against the surface and cover with the lid. Freeze in the coldest part of your freezer until firm, at least 6 hours and preferably overnight.
